Leadership & Involvement

Service, Mentorship, and Faith-Based Leadership

Leadership Philosophy

Leadership, to me, is rooted in service, adaptability, and intentional relationships. Through faith-based environments and mentoring roles, I have learned that effective leadership often means meeting people where they are and remaining flexible in order to guide, support, and challenge others well.

Camp Eagle — Group Camp Counselor

Outdoor Leadership, Mentorship, Program Facilitation

Served as a Group Camp Counselor at Camp Eagle over three summers, supporting a faith-based mission focused on Christ-like character development through outdoor adventure, authentic relationships, and biblical truth. I led middle and high school students through physically and relationally challenging experiences designed to promote growth, resilience, and reflection.

  • Led and supervised groups through high-energy outdoor activities including rock climbing, hiking, and team-based challenge courses
  • Facilitated small group discussions and devotionals that encouraged spiritual growth, self-reflection, and meaningful conversation
  • Learned to treat flexibility as a leadership strategy by adapting plans, communication styles, and expectations to meet the needs of each group
  • Prioritized camper safety, inclusion, and engagement in dynamic and unpredictable environments
  • Developed strong leadership, conflict resolution, and mentoring skills within a faith-driven team setting

First Baptist Church Bryan — College Ministry Leadership

Volunteer Leadership, Discipleship, Community Engagement

Served in multiple leadership roles within the student ministry at First Baptist Church Bryan, contributing to a welcoming, intentional, and Christ-centered environment. Across these roles, I focused on relational leadership, proactive engagement, and ensuring that individuals felt seen, supported, and connected within the church community.

  • Freshman Equip Leader (2024-2026): Led a weekly Bible study for college freshmen focused on biblical discipleship and what it means to lead with integrity, humility, and faith. Facilitated discussions centered on spiritual growth, accountability, and applying biblical principles to leadership and everyday decision-making.
  • Experience Team Member (2023-2026): Served as a welcoming and approachable point of contact for individuals entering the church, emphasizing hospitality and intentional connection. Worked to ensure that no one “fell through the cracks” by helping visitors connect with others before leaving the building.
  • Parking Lot Team Member (2025-2026): Assisted with directing traffic and managing parking flow to ensure safe and efficient entry and exit for attendees. Prioritized safety, clear communication, and a positive first impression through a calm and friendly presence.

Through these roles, I strengthened my communication skills, situational awareness, and ability to lead through service while balancing structure with flexibility in dynamic, people-centered environments.

Engaged in intentional one-on-one discipleship with two college sophomores, focusing on personal growth, accountability, and spiritual development. This experience strengthened my ability to listen well, ask thoughtful questions, and lead through example rather than instruction alone.

  • Gained experience guiding others through challenges while continuing my own growth as a leader and mentor
